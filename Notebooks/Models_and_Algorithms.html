Liver Cancer Prediction: Model Training and Evaluation¶

This script focuses on the training, evaluation, and optimization of Machine Learning models for predicting liver cancer.

The following steps are performed:

  • Loading and preparing the dataset, ensuring compatibility between training and test sets.
  • Comparing different Machine Learning algorithms, including Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Random Forest, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and Gradient Boosting models.
  • Hyperparameter tuning to optimize model performance using GridSearchCV and RandomizedSearchCV.
  • Evaluation of models based on key metrics such as Accuracy, Recall, Precision, F1-Score, and ROC-AUC, with a focus on recall due to the critical nature of detecting liver cancer cases.
  • Final model selection, identifying the best-performing model for accurate liver cancer prediction.

This structured approach ensures that the most effective and reliable model is selected for detecting liver cancer, improving early diagnosis and potential treatment planning.

Model creation¶

In this section, we focus on building and training Machine Learning models for liver cancer prediction. The goal is to evaluate multiple algorithms to determine which one provides the best predictive performance.

The process involves:

  • Defining a set of candidate models, including Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Random Forest, Support Vector Machines (SVM), AdaBoost, and XGBoost.
  • Establishing evaluation metrics to compare models based on their ability to accurately classify liver cancer cases.
  • Implementing cross-validation to ensure robust and unbiased model assessment.

By systematically comparing different approaches, we aim to identify the most effective model for reliable liver cancer detection.

Algorithms comparison¶

In this section, we conduct a comprehensive evaluation of multiple Machine Learning algorithms to identify the most suitable model for liver cancer prediction. By testing different classifiers, we aim to determine which algorithm offers the best balance between accuracy, recall, and robustness.

The comparison includes:

  • Training and validating Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Random Forest, Linear SVC, AdaBoost, and XGBoost models.
  • Using cross-validation to assess model consistency across different data splits.
  • Measuring performance with key metrics such as accuracy, recall, precision, F1-score, and Matthews correlation coefficient (MCC).

This analysis provides critical insights into which models are best suited for the dataset, allowing for an informed decision on the final model selection.

Hyperparameter tuning¶

Once the best-performing algorithms have been identified, the next step is optimizing their hyperparameters to enhance predictive performance. Hyperparameter tuning is a crucial process in fine-tuning model behavior, preventing underfitting or overfitting, and improving generalization.

In this section, we:

  • Utilize RandomizedSearchCV to efficiently explore a range of hyperparameter values for Random Forest and XGBoost models.
  • Optimize key parameters such as number of estimators, learning rate, maximum depth, and class balancing techniques.
  • Evaluate models using recall as the primary scoring metric, ensuring better detection of liver cancer cases.

By refining these hyperparameters, we ensure that the selected models perform optimally, making them more reliable for real-world application.

Training and Evaluation with best parameters¶

After identifying the optimal hyperparameters for the best-performing models, the next step is training and evaluating them to assess their performance on the dataset. This phase ensures that the models not only fit well to the training data but also generalize effectively to unseen cases.

In this section, we:

  • Train Random Forest and XGBoost using the best hyperparameters obtained from the tuning process.
  • Evaluate performance on both the training and test sets, analyzing recall, accuracy, and cross-validation scores.
  • Detect potential overfitting by comparing training and test performance.
  • Use learning curves, confusion matrices, and ROC curves to gain deeper insights into the models’ strengths and weaknesses.

This step is crucial for determining the most reliable and robust model for predicting liver cancer, ensuring that it performs well in practical applications.

Model selection¶

After evaluating the models using multiple performance metrics, the next step is to select the best model for liver cancer prediction. The selection process is based on a combination of factors, including:

  • Performance Metrics: Accuracy, recall, precision, F1-score, and ROC-AUC scores.
  • Generalization Ability: How well the model performs on unseen data, avoiding overfitting.
  • Feature Importance: Evaluating which features contribute most to the model’s predictions.
  • Computational Efficiency: Considering training time and complexity, especially for real-world applications.

By comparing Random Forest and XGBoost, along with their fine-tuned versions, we aim to identify the most effective and reliable model for liver cancer detection. The selected model will be further analyzed and validated to ensure its robustness in real-world scenarios.
